# Competitive Battlecard v1.0

2026-07-15. Grounded in the July 2026 research ([research-annex-2026-07.md](research-annex-2026-07.md)); refresh quarterly with the re-validation ritual and after discovery (objection inventory). Internal use.

## Microsoft (Agent 365 / Copilot Control System / Purview / Copilot Analytics)

- **They're stronger:** native, zero-integration, ships with the licences customers already own; registry/blocking/credits dashboards improving quarterly; unbeatable distribution.
- **We're stronger:** cross-vendor scope beyond registry sync; org-process governance (their controls, our operating model); cost *blending* across licence+consumption+cloud+third-party; evidence-classed value measurement; independence on spend judgment.
- **Why customers buy us:** "Microsoft shows you Microsoft's meters. Nobody blends the meters, attributes them to BUs, and grades the value claims — and no vendor audits itself credibly on *spend-worthiness*."
- **Why they choose Microsoft:** already paid for; security team trusts first-party; "good enough" visibility; E7 shops get Agent 365 anyway.
- **Objections & responses:** *"Wait for Microsoft's roadmap"* → "Their roadmap makes us stronger — every API they ship, we consume (ADR-004); what they'll never ship is the cross-vendor blend and the licence-cut recommendation. Their own admin center flags inactive licences; it will never advocate reducing your Microsoft commitment at renewal — we will, with evidence classes your auditors accept." (Note: do **not** claim Microsoft shows no licence-usage insights — it does; precision here is credibility.) · *"You depend on their APIs"* → show the provider contract + coverage-map honesty: we display what we can't see; nobody else does.

## ServiceNow (AI Control Tower)

- **They're stronger:** bundled into every AI tier (effectively "free" to their AI customers); CMDB anchoring; enterprise workflow credibility; Traceloop observability; huge installed base. **This is our hardest competitive conversation — treat it as such.**
- **We're stronger:** Microsoft-estate depth (evidence-validated feeds: PPAC, Entra Agent ID, credits, Purview) where their coverage is integration-generic; economics honesty machinery (evidence classes, Unattributed, realisable-at-renewal) vs their spend view that press coverage itself called "hazy"; independence from a platform whose AI consumption ("assists") is itself a cost the customer needs audited; buyable without ServiceNow AI-tier commitment.
- **Why customers buy us:** "The governance layer shouldn't be a feature of one of the platforms being governed — especially not one metering its own AI consumption against you."
- **Why they choose ServiceNow:** it's included; one throat to choke; ITSM-centric orgs default to the CMDB worldview.
- **Objections & responses:** *"It's free in our tier"* → "Included isn't independent, and included isn't Microsoft-deep. Ask it to reconcile your Copilot Credits, your EA renewal exposure, and your Foundry token spend into one BU statement with evidence classes — then ask who audits *its* assists consumption." Also: their expanded scope is Innovation-Lab/GA-August maturity in places — ask for reference depth on the Measure pillar specifically.
- **Procurement angle:** we are a second vendor (friction); counter with export/anti-lock-in commitment and the licence-dependency map as consultative value in the sales cycle itself.

## Credo AI

- **They're stronger:** responsible-AI/compliance documentation depth, EU AI Act policy packs, Microsoft partnership optics, analyst presence in AI governance.
- **We're stronger:** operational (live inventory, cost, workflows) vs documentary; system-of-record with evidence trails vs assessments; economics entirely absent from their product.
- **Buy us:** "Credo tells you whether an AI system is *responsible*; we tell you whether your AI estate is *known, governed, and worth it* — and we can feed them evidence."
- **Choose them:** compliance-first buyer (legal/RAI office), especially with the 2027 high-risk wave; regulated-model documentation needs.
- **Response posture:** partner-adjacent, not enemy — position for coexistence ("their policy packs over our ledger" is a roadmap conversation, C2.9 Later), and never compete for the RAI-documentation budget.

## Holistic AI

- **They're stronger:** bias/fairness auditing, EU AI Act risk classification, legal-buyer relationships.
- **We're stronger:** everything operational and economic; they have no Microsoft-estate depth, no cost intelligence, no live governance workflows.
- **Buy us / choose them:** same split as Credo (operational backbone vs compliance assessments); they win legal-led deals we shouldn't chase.
- **Response:** same coexistence posture; if forced head-to-head, the demo is the argument — live ledger vs static assessment.

## Larridin

- **They're stronger:** adoption/impact analytics polish, a16z-backed velocity, token-spend product, "AI impact" narrative executives like; earlier to the value-measurement story.
- **We're stronger:** governance + ledger + economics as one system (their value claims attach to no governed registry); Microsoft-estate depth **unverified on their side** (our research found no documented Copilot/PPAC/Entra depth — a fair diligence question to plant); evidence-class honesty vs impact-dashboard optimism; EU privacy architecture (jurisdiction-scoped levels) vs US-centric telemetry.
- **Buy us:** "Value claims without a governed asset ledger underneath are exactly the dashboards CFOs have stopped believing. Ask any vendor: *validated by whom, against what ledger, at what confidence?*"
- **Choose them:** adoption-analytics-first buyers (HR/transformation offices), lighter security review appetite, US-centric orgs.
- **Objection & response:** *"They're faster/slicker"* → concede polish; the counter is the audit test — "whose numbers go in front of your audit committee?"
